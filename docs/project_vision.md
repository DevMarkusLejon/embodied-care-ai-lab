# Project Vision

Embodied Care AI Lab explores technical prototypes for AI systems that take bodies, biological variation, and care constraints seriously.

Many AI systems treat users as generic individuals. Health and care contexts are different: bodies vary, symptoms can present differently across sex and life stage, care work is often distributed unevenly, and real decisions happen under uncertainty, time pressure, dependency, and institutional constraints.

The repository starts with a modest benchmark for language-model responses to women's health prompts in Swedish and English. Later modules can explore sex-aware medical machine learning, care-load simulation, and assistive robotics concepts.

The project is intended to be:

- empirical rather than rhetorical;
- transparent about limitations;
- safety-oriented;
- suitable for engineering portfolios and research conversations;
- extensible toward embodied AI, robotics, and simulation-to-real care systems.

The goal is not to make broad claims about medicine or society. The goal is to build small, inspectable tools that make model behavior easier to evaluate.
